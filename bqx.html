BQX Tram or Brooklyn-Queens Connector is a light rail project that runs along the East river from Queens to Brooklyn. My High School partnered with BQX to help come up with potential station design concepts. Since the light rail runs along the East river and neighborhoods near the light rail are very industrial, I wanted to reflect that in my design.

I wanted the station to look like a layer of the ground is peeled up. That is why the design features curvers instead of just boring box one sees all around the city. The same design language is carried to the benches as well. They are made to look like they are peeled up from the platform. The center design element of the station also features a beautiful perforated metal structure with a patina finish. This gives the whole station a modern rustic feel which is very common in the industrial areas Brooklyn and Queens. The information screens are mounted on structure that promotes vertical planting. As we all know that big cities are known for their lack of greenery, so this way the plants give a little more life to the station.
